---

**Manas App — Profile Tab Full Rework**

Completely rework the existing Profile tab into a premium, full-scale, user-friendly experience. Do not touch any other tab or screen. The profile should feel like a personal sanctuary — warm, considered, and reflective of the user's journey — not a settings menu.

---

## Overall Design Principles for This Tab

- Generous spacing between all sections — the page should breathe
- Each section lives inside its own soft card, consistent with the dark card treatment used across the rest of the app
- The page is fully scrollable with no clipping or overflow issues
- Every section header should have a small relevant icon to its left
- Subtle visual hierarchy — the most personal and identity-driven content sits at the top, utilities and legal sit at the bottom
- Maintain the existing dark premium aesthetic throughout

---

## Section 1 — User Identity Card

This is the first thing the user sees and must feel personal, warm, and premium:

- Large avatar circle with the user's initial, using the existing lavender gradient treatment
- User's name in large bold text beneath the avatar
- Directly below the name, a **wellness level indicator** — styled like a progression system found in premium games and fitness apps:
  - Display the user's current level name in elegant text — for example: Seedling, Wanderer, Seeker, Practitioner, Sage, Guardian, Luminary. Each level should feel meaningful and aspirational, not gamified or childish
  - Below the level name, a soft horizontal progress bar showing how far along they are toward the next level, with a subtle glow effect on the filled portion
  - The bar should feel premium — thin, refined, with a gentle gradient fill using the app's accent colours
  - Tapping anywhere on this level block expands a small elegant panel beneath it that shows: the current level name and a one-line description of what it represents, the number of wellness logs completed so far, how many more logs are needed to reach the next level, and a preview of the next level name. This panel should animate open and closed smoothly. It should feel like lifting the cover of something — not a popup or modal
  - Level progression is based on total wellness activity logs across all features — journal entries, breathwork sessions, sleep sessions, music sessions, and brain training games all count
- A discreet edit icon in the top-right corner of the card to update the user's name and avatar colour

---

## Section 2 — Manas Premium Card

Positioned just below the identity card, this should feel like a natural next step in the user's journey — not an interruption:

- For free users: a full-width card using a deep, rich gradient in dark purple to indigo tones, consistent with the app palette. A single short line describing what Premium unlocks. A clean, minimal Unlock button. No loud colours, no aggressive sales language. It should feel like a quiet invitation to go deeper.
- For Premium users: replace this entirely with a Premium Member card — show a subtle crown icon, their plan name, and renewal date. This should feel like a badge of belonging, calm and understated.

---

## Section 3 — Wellness Profile

This section brings the profile to life by reflecting the user's personal wellness identity back to them:

- Display their selected wellness goals as soft, elegant pill tags with gentle accent colour borders
- Display their meditation experience level in a single line of muted text
- Display their preferred time of day for wellness practice
- Below all of this, a dedicated **Retake Wellness Quiz** button — styled as a full-width soft card button with a refresh icon and the label "Retake Wellness Quiz" in the app's accent lavender. Beneath the label, a short muted subtext line such as "Refresh your profile and personalise your experience." The button should feel considered and intentional — premium in its restraint, not a plain text link and not an aggressive call to action. When tapped, it takes the user back through the full onboarding quiz and updates their profile on completion.

---

## Section 4 — Account

A clean grouped card with comfortable internal spacing between each row:

- Email address displayed in muted text with a discreet edit icon
- Subscription status — plan name and renewal or expiry date in muted text
- Manage Subscription row with a right-facing chevron
- Restore Purchases row with a right-facing chevron
- Sign Out as the final row in this card — in a muted, understated warm red tone, with a confirmation alert before signing out

---

## Section 5 — Settings

A clean grouped card. Refine the visual treatment to match the premium card style used throughout this redesign:

- **Notifications** — current status shown on the right, tapping opens notification preferences
- **Theme** — two options only: **Dark** and **Light**. Remove the existing Dark, Darker, and Midnight options entirely and replace with just these two. Tapping opens a simple elegant selector showing two preview swatches side by side — one for Dark, one for Light — so the user can see what they are choosing before applying it. The selected theme applies immediately. Theme preference persists across sessions.
- **Daily Reminder** — current time shown on the right, tapping opens a time picker

---

## Section 6 — Crisis & Mental Health Resources

This section must feel like a quiet, warm presence — never alarming, never clinical:

- A soft card with a very subtle warm tint to distinguish it gently from the surrounding cards
- A short warm opening line such as "You are not alone. Support is always available."
- Three tappable resource rows beneath it, each with an icon, name, and a brief one-line descriptor:
  - **Crisis Text Line** — Free and confidential support, available 24 hours
  - **International Association for Suicide Prevention** — Global directory of crisis support centres
  - **Breathing Reset** — A shortcut that takes the user directly into the Physiological Sigh session in the Breathe tab as an immediate in-app grounding tool
- The first two rows open their respective external links in the device browser with a gentle confirmation prompt before leaving the app
- The third row navigates internally with no prompt needed
- This card should never feel heavy or alarming — it should feel like a hand on the shoulder

Each row navigates to its own dedicated in-app screen. Each screen should be fully built and structured — with the section title as the page header and a clean content area ready to receive copy. Placeholder text is fine for now but the screen must feel complete and consistent with the app's design language, not a blank white page.

## Section 8 — Delete Account

Positioned below the Legal & Support card, separated by enough space to feel deliberate and considered:

- A single quietly styled row — not red, not alarming. Muted and understated, treating the user like an adult
- Tapping triggers a two-step confirmation flow: first a calm alert asking if they are sure, then a second confirmation making clear the action is permanent
- After confirmation, all user state is cleared and the app returns to the onboarding flow

## Footer

The Manas logo and version number sit at the very bottom of the scrollable page with generous padding above them. This should feel like a quiet signature at the end of a considered experience.

## Light Theme — Global Application

When the user selects Light from the theme selector, the following applies across the entire app:

- The base background shifts to a warm off-white with a very subtle lavender tint — soft and airy, never harsh or clinical
- Cards shift from dark grey to soft warm white with a delicate shadow to maintain depth and layering
- All accent colours remain consistent but at slightly deeper saturation to maintain contrast against the lighter background
- Text shifts from off-white to deep navy or charcoal
- The bottom navigation bar and all screen headers follow the same light treatment
- The overall feeling should be like soft morning light coming through frosted glass — warm, premium, and considered
