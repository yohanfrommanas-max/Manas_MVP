A letter from your past
Written 22 September 2025 · Six months ago
Dear future self,

I am writing this on a Sunday morning in September. The light is coming through the window at an angle that will not exist again until next year.

By the time you read this, I hope you have made peace with the things that are keeping me awake tonight. The work uncertainty. The distance from people I love. The question of whether I am becoming the person I set out to be.

I want you to know that right now, in this moment, I am trying. That is enough. I hope you remember that when the trying gets harder.

I have been reading Marcus Aurelius again. He reminds me that each day is both complete and temporary. I am practicing that.

What has changed? What has stayed true? Tell me when you write your next one.

With honesty and hope,
You, on 22 September 2025

Friday, 20 March · 7:14 am
On the discipline of perception
Calm MindfulnessLearningSpiritual

The morning walk revealed something I had long forgotten — that clarity is not found, it is cultivated through steady attention to what is actually present.

I passed the same streets I have passed a hundred times. And yet today, by the choice to simply observe rather than interpret, everything felt new. The old oak. The man reading at the corner table. The quality of the light before eight.

This is what Epictetus means by the discipline of assent — to withhold judgment long enough to actually see. Not to react to the impression of a thing, but to the thing itself.

I want to carry this practice into the rest of the day. Especially in the meeting this afternoon, where my impulse has always been to interpret before I listen.

Evening Reflection
"I did hold judgment at bay in the afternoon. Not perfectly. But more than before. Progress is sufficient."

Thursday, 19 March · 9:02 pm
What is within my control
Restless WorkChallengesGoals

I returned to the dichotomy again today. The meeting did not go as I had hoped, yet I found myself clinging to outcomes I cannot shape.

What can I actually control? My preparation. My attitude. My words in the moment. Nothing more. The decision was never mine to own.

Marcus writes: "Very little is needed to make a happy life; it is all within yourself, in your way of thinking." I copy this out. I will read it again tomorrow.

Stoic Reminder
"Make the best use of what is in your power, and take the rest as it happens." — Epictetus
